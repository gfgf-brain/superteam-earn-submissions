# Write an X Thread: Advice on Surviving Your First Hackathon

Here's a high-quality 10-tweet Twitter/X thread for Superteam Earn on surviving your first hackathon:

---

1/10
Did you know over 70% of first-time hackathon participants don't finish their projects? 🤯 Don't be a statistic! Surviving your first hackathon is an art. Here's your battle plan. 🧵👇

2/10
**1. Pre-Hackathon Prep is Key:** Don't just show up! Research the theme, understand the sponsor challenges, and even brainstorm potential ideas *before* day one. Familiarity breeds speed. 💡

3/10
**2. Team Up Wisely:** Find teammates with complementary skills (dev, design, biz dev). A cohesive team is more than the sum of its parts. Look for passion and good communication. 🤝

4/10
**3. Scope Ruthlessly:** Your MVP (Minimum Viable Product) is your best friend. Focus on ONE core feature that solves a problem. Don't try to build the next Facebook in 48 hours. 🎯

5/10
**4. Leverage Existing Tools & APIs:** Don't reinvent the wheel! Utilize libraries, SDKs, and pre-built components. For Solana, explore @solana's dev tools & @Chainlink's oracles. Speed is everything. ⚡️

6/10
**5. Embrace the "Done is Better Than Perfect" Mindset:** Perfectionism is the enemy of hackathon completion. Get a functional prototype working, even if it's rough around the edges. You can polish later. ✨

7/10
**6. Prioritize Sleep & Nutrition (Seriously!):** Burning out is real. Short naps, healthy snacks, and staying hydrated will keep your brain sharp. Your code will thank you. 🍎💧

8/10
**7. Communicate Constantly:** Regular stand-ups (even brief ones) keep everyone aligned. Use tools like Discord or Slack effectively. Address blockers immediately. 🗣️

9/10
**8. Practice Your Pitch:** You've built something awesome, now sell it! Clearly articulate the problem, your solution, and its impact. A compelling pitch can make or break your project. 🎤

10/10
Surviving your first hackathon is about smart planning, execution, and resilience. Focus on your MVP, leverage resources, and communicate! Ready to build?
